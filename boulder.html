Boulder outdoor venues

Where the Flatirons meet the festival crowd

Boulder's outdoor event culture is sophisticated — so are its attendees. A crowd that spends the rest of their year connected to gigabit internet notices immediately when the event WiFi can't handle tap-to-pay. Fieldcast brings enterprise-grade connectivity to every corner of Boulder's outdoor event scene.

Park / Historic
Chautauqua Park

Boulder's iconic 1898 cultural landmark at the base of the Flatirons. Concerts, community events, and yoga festivals draw large crowds into a setting where cellular can't keep pace with demand. Fieldcast deploys at the meadow perimeter and covers the full event footprint.

Festival / Downtown
Pearl Street Mall

Boulder's pedestrian heart hosts outdoor markets, festivals, and seasonal events along several blocks of closed-off street. High-density vendor POS and heavy foot traffic make cellular unreliable during peak events. We bring a dedicated uplink that doesn't compete with 10,000 phones.

Festival / Creek
Boulder Creek Festival Grounds

The Boulder Creek Path corridor and adjacent festival grounds host Boulder's largest annual outdoor events. Linear footprint, multiple stages, scattered vendor zones — Fieldcast covers the full length with access points positioned for consistent coverage across the spread-out site.

Race / Athletic
Boulder Reservoir

Home to Boulder Ironman, open water swims, and triathlon events. The reservoir's open-sky position is perfect for Starlink uplink — and the remote location makes cellular essentially nonexistent during event peak. Timing systems, medical stations, and registration all need Fieldcast.

University / Outdoor
CU Boulder Campus Grounds

Commencement on Folsom Field, outdoor concerts, and large campus events push beyond the university WiFi footprint. Fieldcast supplements with a dedicated temporary network for guest attendees, vendor operations, and production crews — invisible to the campus network, fully managed.

Open Space
Walker Ranch / Open Space

Boulder's extensive open space network hosts trail running events, mountain bike races, and outdoor retreats that push well beyond any cell tower's useful range. A single Fieldcast deployment covers aid stations, race timing, and emergency coordination across miles of course.

Why Boulder

Boulder's event crowd is tech-savvy. The outdoor venues aren't.

Boulder has one of the highest concentrations of software engineers, researchers, and startup founders in the country. When they attend an outdoor event, they expect the same seamless connectivity they have everywhere else. When POS fails or the WiFi badge scan times out, they notice — and they say something.

The problem is structural. Boulder's most beloved outdoor event venues — Chautauqua, the creek corridor, the reservoir — weren't designed with networking infrastructure in mind. The open space that makes them beautiful is the same open space that made cellular tower placement difficult or politically contentious.

Fieldcast brings the network to the event rather than waiting for infrastructure that will never arrive. We serve the full Boulder–Longmont corridor and are frequently booked for events the same week they're planned.
