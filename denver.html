Denver outdoor venues

Serving the Front Range's biggest outdoor spaces

Denver's outdoor event scene runs year-round — from summer festivals at City Park to autumn markets on the 16th Street Mall. Every one of these venues sits beyond the reach of reliable cellular, and none of them have run conduit to the grass. That's what Fieldcast is built for.

9,000-seat natural amphitheater carved into sandstone at 6,450 feet. Cellular is consistently saturated on event nights. Fieldcast provides a dedicated managed network for production crews, vendor POS, and backstage operations — independent of the overloaded carrier towers.

330 acres in the heart of Denver — home to the Denver Jazz Festival, PrideFest, and dozens of permitted events. The park's open-sky footprint is ideal for Starlink uplink, and our access points cover the full event zone without cable runs.

Why Denver

The Mile High City runs on outdoor events. The coverage doesn't keep up.

Denver hosts more permitted outdoor events per capita than almost any city in the west — and the cellular infrastructure at most of those venues was designed for a normal day in the park, not 8,000 people all trying to pay by card at once.

The problem isn't the carriers. It's that a tower optimized for a neighborhood of 2,000 people is suddenly serving a festival of 10,000 — and bandwidth math doesn't work in anyone's favor. POS drops out. Registration apps time out. Livestream uplinks stall.

Fieldcast brings a completely independent uplink — satellite-based, not tied to any carrier network. No matter how saturated the local towers get, your event's critical systems stay connected. We serve everything from intimate rooftop corporate events to 10,000-person festivals across the metro.
