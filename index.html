A dead zone used to mean a different site. Not anymore.

Connectivity failure at an outdoor event isn't just frustrating — it's a direct revenue hit. Point-of-sale systems go offline and vendor lines grind to cash-only. Attendee registration tablets stop syncing. Staff radios fall back to shouting. Live streams drop. And whoever organized the event takes the blame for every single one of those failures.

Fieldcast removes that risk entirely. We park a fully equipped Starlink station at your site, raise the access points, and have broadband broadcasting across a 500-meter radius — covering up to 5,000 sq ft of active event space — before your first attendee walks through the gate. No ISP lead time. No generator rental. No cable runs. No site negotiation about power access.

The rig is a Ford Transit Extended loaded with 800 watts of roof-mounted solar, a 10 kWh lithium battery bank, and enterprise-grade wireless infrastructure — the same hardware category used by large-scale outdoor productions, made fully mobile. Starlink's low-earth-orbit satellite network provides the uplink, delivering real broadband in locations where a cell tower has never been built and fiber will never be run.

Network management

Not just a hotspot. A managed network built around your event.

Most temporary WiFi solutions are a consumer hotspot in a weatherproof case. Fieldcast runs enterprise network hardware — which means you control who gets what bandwidth, on which network, with your branding attached to it. These are the capabilities that separate a real network from a workaround.

Custom Branded SSIDs
Your event, your network name. We configure SSIDs to match your event or sponsor branding — attendees connect to FestivalName-WiFi or SponsorName-Guest, not a generic network. Up to 8 simultaneous named networks from a single deployment.
Included — Professional & above
Per-Device Bandwidth Caps
Set a ceiling on how much bandwidth any single attendee device can consume. A guest streaming a video doesn't degrade the experience for 200 other people. Caps are configurable per SSID — public WiFi gets capped, POS terminals get uncapped dedicated throughput.
Configurable per SSID
Traffic Prioritization (QoS)
Point-of-sale and registration traffic is always first. Our QoS configuration ensures that payment processing, attendee check-in, and staff comms have guaranteed throughput even when the public network is at peak load. Critical operations never compete with casual browsing.
POS & ops traffic protected
Captive Portal & Splash Page
When attendees join your public WiFi, they land on a custom-branded splash page before getting access — your logo, event info, sponsor recognition, or terms of use. Optional access codes for VIP networks. No third-party login required.
Branded & configurable
Network Isolation & VLANs
Staff, vendors, and attendees operate on completely separate network segments. A vendor on the POS network can't see attendee devices. Your production crew's VLAN is invisible to the public. Isolation is enforced at the hardware level — not a firewall rule that can be circumvented.
Hardware-level isolation
Live Monitoring Dashboard
Our on-site technician watches a live dashboard showing connected device count, per-SSID throughput, signal strength at coverage edges, and satellite link health. If something changes — a traffic spike, a device overloading the uplink — we see it before you do and respond immediately.
Real-time, on-site response

Coverage & limitations
Buildings & enclosed structures WiFi signals do not reliably penetrate solid building walls, thick concrete, or enclosed structures. Fieldcast is designed for open-air and outdoor deployments. Indoor coverage in nearby structures is not guaranteed and may require additional hardware.
Metal structures & vehicles Large metal objects — trailers, shipping containers, stage rigs, food truck clusters — can attenuate or block signal in their immediate shadow. We account for these during site planning; additional access points can be added to address known obstructions.
Satellite uplink requirements Starlink requires a clear view of the sky to operate. Dense tree canopy, canyon terrain, or locations with significant overhead obstruction can affect signal quality. We assess sky coverage during planning and will advise if a site presents uplink risk.
Speed & device count Real-world throughput and concurrent device capacity vary based on satellite conditions, terrain, device density, and network usage patterns. Published figures represent typical performance under standard outdoor conditions and are not guaranteed.
